# Qualification Criteria — B2B SaaS (Generic)

**Purpose:** Defines what "qualified" looks like for a B2B SaaS deal at each stage.
Used by analysis subagents to assess deal health and flag qualification gaps.

**Customize this file** to match your company's sales motion, segment definitions,
and qualification standards.

---

## What Qualified Looks Like

A qualified deal has clear, evidence-based answers to these questions:

1. **Is there real pain?** Not curiosity — a business problem with measurable consequences.
2. **Is there budget authority?** Someone who can approve the spend is identified and engaged.
3. **Is there a decision process?** We know how they evaluate vendors and who's involved.
4. **Is there urgency?** A reason this gets decided *this quarter* vs. next year.
5. **Are we solving the right problem?** Our product maps to their actual need, not a nearby one.
6. **Do we have a champion?** Someone internal to the prospect who wants us to win.

## Stage Definitions

### Discovery

**Goal:** Determine if there's a real problem we can solve, and whether it's worth investing in.

**Minimum to advance:**
- Pain identified and quantified (not just acknowledged)
- Relevant buyer persona confirmed (are we talking to someone who can evaluate this seriously?)
- Technical feasibility not blocked (no obvious showstopper)
- Prospect has agreed to a next step with a specific date

**Red flags at discovery:**
- Prospect can't articulate why they're looking now
- No one on the call has budget visibility
- Prospect is "just researching" with no defined timeline
- We're talking to too-junior a contact with no path to the buyer

---

### Technical Evaluation

**Goal:** Confirm our product works for their use case. Establish technical champion.

**Minimum to advance:**
- Technical requirements documented and mapped to our capabilities
- Key technical decision-maker identified and engaged
- Proof of concept / trial scope agreed
- No blocking technical gaps (or blockers have a clear resolution path)

**Red flags at technical eval:**
- SE can't get a technical contact to engage
- Requirements keep expanding (scope creep before we've even started)
- Prospect is evaluating 4+ vendors simultaneously with no shortlisting plan
- Security/compliance requirements surface that we can't meet

---

### Champion Building / Business Case

**Goal:** Build internal momentum. Get access to the economic buyer. Construct the business case they'll use internally.

**Minimum to advance:**
- Champion identified — name, title, and confirmed motivations
- Economic buyer identified (even if not yet directly engaged)
- Business case framed (ROI, cost reduction, risk reduction, or strategic value)
- Decision criteria documented — what they'll use to choose a vendor
- Competitive position understood — who else is in the deal

**Red flags at champion/business case:**
- Champion goes quiet after meetings — low internal momentum
- We've never been able to get past the champion to an EB
- Decision criteria shifts repeatedly — internal misalignment
- Competitor has a stronger champion than we do

---

### Late Stage / Commercial

**Goal:** Close. Navigate legal, procurement, and final objections.

**Minimum to advance to closed:**
- MSA / legal review initiated
- Pricing agreed in principle (within range)
- Economic buyer has given verbal commitment
- Procurement / security reviews have started
- Clear close date agreed by both sides

**Red flags at late stage:**
- Legal review stalls without explanation
- New stakeholders appear after commercial terms seemed settled
- Champion becomes unavailable or disengaged
- Pricing suddenly reopened after verbal agreement

## Scoring Anchors

### MEDDPICC Weight by Stage

| Stage | Most Critical Components |
|-------|------------------------|
| Discovery | Identified Pain, Metrics (baseline), Champion (initial signal) |
| Technical Eval | Decision Criteria, Competition, Identified Pain (technical) |
| Champion/BC | Economic Buyer, Decision Process, Champion (health check) |
| Late Stage | Paper Process, Economic Buyer (re-confirm), Decision Criteria (final) |

---

## Deal Health Scoring Guide

**80-100 — Strong**
- Pain clearly identified and quantified
- Champion named and engaged
- Decision process mapped
- Clear urgency driver
- Next steps are specific and mutual

**60-79 — Moderate**
- Pain identified but not fully quantified
- Champion possible but not confirmed
- Decision process partially understood
- Timeline is soft ("this quarter" vs. specific date)

**40-59 — At Risk**
- Pain is acknowledged but vague
- No champion identified
- Decision process unknown
- No urgency driver
- Next steps are one-sided (seller-driven only)

**0-39 — Critical**
- Multiple missing qualification components
- Deal stalled with no agreed next step
- Champion disengaged or absent
- Significant competitive threat with no differentiation

## Common Disqualification Signals

These patterns warrant a direct qualification conversation with the prospect:

- **"We're just researching"** with no defined project, budget, or timeline
- **"Send me a proposal"** before discovery is complete
- **No access to economic buyer** after 3+ meetings
- **Timelines keep slipping** — each meeting reveals a new delay
- **Competitor is deeply embedded** with no compelling event driving a switch
- **Requirements we can't meet** and prospect won't accept a workaround
- **Internal champion leaves** the company or changes roles mid-deal

## Notes for Analysis Subagents

When scoring MEDDPICC and deal health:

1. **Use call evidence only.** Don't assume information that wasn't surfaced on the call.
2. **Prior calls don't carry forward.** Each brief reflects what was learned on this call.
3. **"Not addressed" is information.** A discovery call that doesn't surface Metrics is a gap.
4. **Stage determines what's expected.** Missing Economic Buyer in early discovery is normal.
   Missing Economic Buyer in late stage is a red flag.
5. **Partial credit is real.** "Partially" means something was touched but not fully established.
   It's not a binary.
